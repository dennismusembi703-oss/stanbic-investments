Crypto Mining

Crypto mining is the process of validating transactions and securing a blockchain network by solving complex cryptographic puzzles using specialized computer hardware. Miners compete to find the correct answer first; the winner adds the next block of transactions to the chain and earns the block reward.

For Bitcoin, that reward is currently 3.125 BTC per block. Mining is essential for maintaining the integrity and security of proof-of-work blockchains because it ensures that only valid transactions are recorded and makes the blockchain extremely difficult to alter or manipulate.

The principle of decentralization is key: distributed miners across the network contribute computational power, preventing any single party from controlling the ledger. Mining underpins security, consensus, and the issuance of new cryptocurrency.

How it works:
Miners solve cryptographic puzzles (hashing) to propose a new block.
Reward:
Block reward + transaction fees (example: 3.125 BTC for Bitcoin).
Security:
Proof-of-work makes rewriting history computationally impractical.
Decentralization:
Multiple independent miners protect the network from censorship and manipulation.
