What is cryptocurrency mining and how does it work?

Charles Archer
11 minutes read

Introduction

Cryptocurrency mining is the backbone of blockchain networks. It involves using powerful computers to solve complex puzzles to verify transactions and secure the system. Our guide breaks down how mining works, the role of rewards and the importance of decentralisation.

What is cryptocurrency mining?

Cryptocurrency mining is the process of verifying and recording transactions on a blockchain using advanced computing power. Miners compete to solve complex mathematical puzzles that validate each new block of transactions. The first miner to solve the puzzle gets to add the new block to the blockchain and is usually rewarded with newly created cryptocurrency and transaction fees.

Mining is essential for maintaining the integrity and security of blockchain networks, as it ensures that only valid transactions are recorded. It makes the blockchain extremely difficult to alter, manipulate or use for fraudulent purposes.

The principle of decentralisation is key to cryptocurrency mining. Unlike traditional fiat money systems, which rely on central authorities like banks, cryptocurrencies operate on peer-to-peer networks. This means that anyone with the right computer system can become a miner and help run the network. This decentralisation of power among thousands of participants worldwide helps to keep each blockchain transparent and resistant to censorship or control.

How does crypto mining work?

Crypto mining plays an essential role in validating and securing blockchain transactions. When someone sends cryptocurrency (for example, Bitcoin) from one wallet to another, the transaction isn’t immediate as it first needs to be verified by the network.

Miners group proposed transactions into blocks. Each block must be validated through an energy intensive process called Proof of Work (PoW), which involves a ‘race’ to solve a complex cryptographic puzzle. The first miner to solve the puzzle broadcasts their solution to the rest of the network, and if verified as correct, the new block is added to the blockchain.

Each block contains a unique cryptographic hash of the previous block, along with its own data and timestamp. This creates a hash-linked chain where altering a single block changes its hash and breaks the link with the next block. Therefore, altering past transactions requires re-mining every subsequent block, which is practically impossible for large networks.

How do crypto miners make money?

Crypto miners earn rewards in two main ways:

  • Block rewards – newly created cryptocurrency awarded to the miner who validates and adds a new block.
  • Transaction fees – fees paid by users which are collected by the miner who includes the transactions in a block.

Factors affecting profitability include block reward halving, mining difficulty, electricity costs, hardware efficiency and operational scale.

Advantages of crypto mining

  • Passive income potential.
  • Support network security and integrity.
  • Earn crypto directly rather than buying.
  • Hands-on participation for technically inclined users.

Disadvantages of crypto mining

  • Environmental impact due to energy consumption.
  • High upfront and operational costs.
  • Technical and operational challenges (heat, noise).
  • Regulatory and security concerns.

Proof of Stake (PoS) vs Proof of Work (PoW)

PoS selects validators based on the amount of cryptocurrency they stake, consuming significantly less energy than PoW. PoS lowers the barrier to entry but may raise centralisation concerns. PoW remains the original consensus mechanism, relying on computational work to secure the network.

How to start mining cryptocurrencies

  1. Choose a cryptocurrency to mine.
  2. Select mining hardware (ASICs or GPUs).
  3. Install mining software (e.g., CGMiner, NiceHash, PhoenixMiner).
  4. Join a mining pool for more consistent rewards.
  5. Set up a wallet to receive rewards.
  6. Calculate profitability using online tools.

Different methods of mining

ASIC, GPU, CPU and cloud mining each have trade-offs in cost, efficiency and flexibility.

Is crypto mining worth it?

Profitability depends on hardware costs, electricity, difficulty, and market prices. Use profitability calculators before investing. Mining profits can be volatile and influenced by market conditions and network changes.

Essential terminology

Hash rate: computational power. Mining difficulty: puzzle hardness. Block time: average time to mine a block.

Gas & Fees — processing withdrawn mined assets

When you move mined cryptocurrency from a mining wallet or pool to your personal (local) wallet, that transfer itself is a blockchain transaction and will incur network fees. The exact mechanics depend on the blockchain:

  • Native-token fees: Most networks require the fee to be paid in the chain’s native token (e.g., ETH for Ethereum). For ERC‑20 or other tokens on Ethereum, gas must be paid in ETH even if you transfer a token.
  • Miner/pool handling: Mining pools and services often manage payouts for you; they may batch many small payouts together to reduce total fees, or deduct a network fee from your payout amount.
  • Fee variability: Network congestion affects the base fee and the tip (priority fee). Higher congestion means higher fees to move funds quickly.
  • Minimum payout economics: Small mined balances can be uneconomical to withdraw if the network fee is similar to or larger than the payout. Pools commonly set a minimum payout threshold.
  • Cross-chain / bridges: Moving assets across chains or using bridges adds extra costs (bridge fees, relayer fees, and additional on‑chain gas).

Practical tips:

  • Check current gas estimators (e.g., ETH Gas Station, Blocknative) before withdrawing to choose an economical time or fee level.
  • Use pools or services that batch payouts to save on per‑withdrawal fees.
  • Keep a small native‑token balance if you hold tokens on chains that require native gas (for example, keep some ETH to pay ERC‑20 transfer fees).
  • Consider off‑chain or layer‑2 options where available — these can reduce fees and make small withdrawals practical.

Outlook

Mining is evolving with greater focus on energy efficiency, PoS adoption, regulatory scrutiny and sustainable practices. Innovations aim to make mining more scalable and environmentally responsible.

FAQs
